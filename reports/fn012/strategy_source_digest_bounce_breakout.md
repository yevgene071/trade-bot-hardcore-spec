### Bounce summary

The current architecture already captures the core hard gates: density/cluster anchor, tape fade, leader contra filter, short stop, news blackout, approach count, and no-progress/partial-exit mechanics. The largest gaps are subpattern-specific: false-break 5m candle confirmation, non-density candle/volume stop anchors, “price did not reach level” spot/futures mapping, detailed 5/10-part exit ladders, and contra-print dominance rather than broad `TapeFade` proxy.

The current `BreakoutEatThrough` model matches the central source rule: density eating + tape burst + support behind + leader neutrality/approval + quick invalidation. ## 4. Traceability gaps / recommended follow-up work

1. **Formal false-break bounce detector**: 5m candle reclaim under/over level, spike/high-low stop anchor, consolidation continuation rejection.
2. **Preparation classifier for breakout**: slow approach + protorgovka width 0.3–2%, cascade/trendline levels, vertical volume increase.
3. **Multi-leg entry and exit plans**: source-specific 3–5 entry legs and 3/10 + 5/10 + 2/10 breakout exits / 5-part bounce exits.
4. **Spot/futures normalized mapping**: source requires spot/futures equal levels ±5 points and fьюч “резиновее”; must use explicit MetaScalp instrument identity before implementation.
5. **Contra-print and tiny alternating-print tape features**: current `TapeFade`/`TapeBurst` are useful proxies but do not fully encode source text about prosadki and small +/- prints.
6. **High-break vs low-break exit policy**: lows should close faster; highs may allow runner when leader/market supports.
